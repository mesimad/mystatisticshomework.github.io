If n independent values are drawn from some distribution and ordered from smallest to largest, the kth value is the kth order statistic. The distribution of this value depends on both the underlying distribution and the size of the sample.

Research 11_R:

"Do a research about the general correlation coefficient for ranks and the most common indices that can be derived by it. Do one example of computation of these correlation coefficients for ranks."


In statistics, correlation coefficient is a numerical measure of some type of correlation, meaning a statistical relationship between two variables. The variables may be two columns of a given data set of observations, often called a sample, or two components of a multivariate random variable with a known distribution.

Regarding ranks, however, we can compute the rank correlation coefficient, which is simply the measure between the ranking of two different variables. The most common rank correlation coefficients are:

Spearman’s rank correlation coefficient

The Kendall tau rank correlation coefficient

All these coefficients have values ranging from -1 to 1, where 0 means that the two variables aren’t correlated at all, while 1 means the variables are perfectly correlated and -1 that they are perfectly correlated, but with reverse values.
